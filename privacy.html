Privacy Policy

01Information We Collect

Kindergarden collects information that you voluntarily provide to us when you enroll your child, contact us through our website, schedule a campus tour, subscribe to our newsletter, or participate in school events. The types of information we collect include:

  • Personal Identification Information: Full name, home address, email address, phone number, date of birth, and government-issued identification of parents or guardians.
  • Student Information: Child's full name, date of birth, medical history, immunization records, allergies, dietary restrictions, developmental assessments, academic records, and any individualized education plans (IEPs) or special accommodations.
  • Emergency Contact Information: Names, phone numbers, and relationships of designated emergency contacts and authorized pickup persons.
  • Payment Information: Billing address, credit or debit card details, bank account information for automatic payments, and tuition payment history. Payment data is processed through PCI-compliant third-party processors and is not stored on our servers.
  • Communications: Records of correspondence with our staff, including emails, phone call logs, and messages sent through our parent portal or communication platforms.
  • Website Usage Data: IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URLs, pages viewed, time spent on pages, and interactions with our website features. This data is collected through cookies and similar technologies as described in Section 04.

We collect this information through enrollment forms, our website contact forms, newsletter sign-ups, event registration pages, parent-teacher conference scheduling, and direct communications with our administrative and teaching staff.

02How We Use Your Information

Kindergarden uses the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To process and manage your child's enrollment, including maintaining student records, scheduling classes, and coordinating with teachers and specialists.
  • To communicate important information about school operations, including schedule changes, emergency closures, event announcements, and academic progress reports.
  • To provide individualized educational support, including tailoring instruction to your child's learning needs, implementing accommodations, and tracking developmental milestones.
  • To process tuition and fee payments, manage billing accounts, and issue tax receipts or financial records as required.
  • To ensure the safety and security of all students, staff, and visitors through our campus access control systems, emergency contact protocols, and health record management.
  • To improve our educational programs, website experience, and parent communications through analysis of usage patterns, feedback, and satisfaction surveys.
  • To comply with legal obligations, including reporting requirements to state education authorities, health departments, and child protective services as mandated by applicable law.
  • To send you marketing communications about upcoming events, enrollment periods, and school programs, only with your explicit consent. You may opt out at any time by clicking the unsubscribe link in any marketing email or contacting our office.

Our Promise: We use your information only for purposes that directly benefit your family's experience at Kindergarden. We never sell your personal information to third parties, and we never use student data for advertising or marketing purposes.

03Data Sharing & Disclosure

Kindergarden does not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. We share information only in the following circumstances:

  • Service Providers: We engage trusted third-party vendors who assist us in operating our school, processing payments, managing our communication platforms, and analyzing website performance. These providers are contractually bound to protect your data and may use it only for the specific services they perform on our behalf.
  • Legal Compliance: We may disclose information when required by law, such as in response to a valid court order, subpoena, or government request. This includes mandatory reporting of suspected child abuse or neglect to appropriate authorities.
  • Educational Transfers: If your child transfers to another educational institution, we will share relevant academic records and developmental assessments with the receiving school upon your written authorization.
  • Emergency Situations: We may share information with medical professionals, law enforcement, or emergency responders in situations involving imminent risk to a child's health or safety.
  • School Community: Directory information (child's name, class, and year group) may be shared within the school community for purposes such as class directories and parent group communications, unless you have opted out in writing.

We require all third parties who handle your data to maintain confidentiality and security standards consistent with our own policies and applicable data protection laws.

04Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience, analyze site traffic, and understand how visitors interact with our content. Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your web browser.

We use the following types of cookies:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the basic functionality of our website, including navigation, form submission, and secure access to the parent portal. These cookies cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics Cookies: We use Google Analytics and similar services to collect anonymous data about how visitors use our site — which pages are most visited, how users navigate between pages, and which devices are used. This helps us improve our website design and content.
  • Preference Cookies: These remember your preferences, such as language settings, font size, and previously viewed content, to provide a more personalized experience.
  • Marketing Cookies: Used only with your explicit consent to deliver relevant promotional content about Kindergarden events and programs on third-party platforms.

You can control cookie preferences through your browser settings. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. For more information about how Google Analytics handles data, visit Google's Privacy Policy at policies.google.com/privacy.

05Data Security

Kindergarden implements industry-standard security measures to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Our security practices include:

  • Encryption of sensitive data in transit using TLS/SSL protocols (indicated by HTTPS in your browser's address bar).
  • Secure, access-controlled servers and databases with regular security audits and vulnerability assessments.
  • Strict access controls ensuring that only authorized personnel with a legitimate need can view or process personal information.
  • Regular staff training on data protection, privacy best practices, and secure handling of student records.
  • PCI-compliant payment processing through accredited third-party gateways. We do not store complete credit card numbers on our systems.
  • Data backup and disaster recovery procedures to prevent loss of critical information.

While we take every reasonable precaution to protect your data, no method of electronic storage or transmission is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, but we will promptly notify you in the event of a data breach that affects your personal information as required by applicable law.

06Children's Privacy

Kindergarden is committed to protecting the privacy of children. We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and all applicable laws regarding the collection of data from minors.

  • We collect personal information from children (students) only through parent-authorized enrollment forms and with the express consent of a parent or legal guardian.
  • We do not require children to disclose more information than is reasonably necessary to participate in our educational programs or school activities.
  • Photographs and videos of children are captured only for educational records, internal assessments, and school communications. Use of student images in public-facing materials (website, social media, brochures) requires separate parental consent and may be revoked at any time.
  • Parents have the right to review, update, or request deletion of their child's personal information at any time by contacting our privacy office.
  • We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 through our website without verifiable parental consent. If we discover that we have inadvertently collected such information, we will delete it promptly.

Your Child's Safety Is Our Priority: If you believe that your child's information has been collected without your consent, please contact us immediately at privacy@kindergarden.edu, and we will take immediate steps to investigate and resolve the issue.

07Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right to Access: You may request a copy of the personal information we hold about you and your child.
  • Right to Rectification: You may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Right to Deletion: You may request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal retention requirements and legitimate business needs such as maintaining student records as required by state education authorities.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You may request that we limit how we use your personal information in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Data Portability: You may request a structured, machine-readable copy of your data for transfer to another organization.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before the withdrawal.
  • Right to Opt Out of Marketing: You may unsubscribe from marketing communications at any time by clicking the unsubscribe link in our emails or contacting our office directly.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information in Section 09. We will respond to your request within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before processing certain requests. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights.

08Updates to This Policy

Kindergarden reserves the right to update or modify this Privacy Policy at any time to reflect changes in our practices, legal obligations, or the regulatory landscape. When we make material changes, we will notify you by:

  • Posting the updated policy on this page with a revised "Last updated" date at the top.
  • Sending an email notification to the primary email address on file for each enrolled family.
  • Displaying a prominent notice on our website for at least 30 days following the change.

We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Your continued use of our services or your child's continued enrollment after any modifications constitutes your acknowledgment and acceptance of the updated policy. If you do not agree with any changes, you may contact us to discuss your options, including withdrawal of enrollment.
